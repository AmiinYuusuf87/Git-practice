The Problem (Life Before VCS)
The Mess: Managing folders with confusing copies 
like project_final_v2_REALLY_final.txt.

The Pain: No reliable history, 
high risk of overwriting teammate's work, 
zero accountability ("who changed what"), and extreme fear of breaking code.

What is a Version Control System (VCS)?
Definition: A smart, automatic time machine 
and undo button for your entire project that records changes over time, 
enabling seamless teamwork.


Centralized vs. Distributed VCS
Centralized (e.g., SVN):

History lives only on one central server.

The Catch: If the server goes down or dies, 
you cannot save work, view history, 
and your data can be lost forever. Requires internet.

Distributed (e.g., Git):

Every single developer has a full copy of the entire history on their computer.

The Win: Works 100% offline, incredibly fast, and if the main server dies, 
any developer's PC can restore the entire project history.

Why Git Won the Tech World
Created in 2005 by Linus Torvalds (the genius behind Linux) to handle massive codebases.

Distributed & Fast: Everything runs locally on your PC.

Lightweight Branching: Lets you experiment on separate lines of work safely.

Resilient: Zero single-point-of-failure risks.

Git was created in 2005 by Linus Torvalds to manage the Linux kernel.
